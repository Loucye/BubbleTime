Bubble Tea

"Weird", "strange", "atypical" or simply "why?" are the words that could come to your mind when seeing those different bubble tea creations above however, they do exist and can actually be to your taste if you already like bubble tea in general.


The bubble tea pizza can be found in Canada, consisting of tapioca dough, mozzarella cheese, topped with brown sugar and a condensed milk glaze. It's a new take on the sweet and salty combo that many people like!

The bubble tea hotpot can also be found in Canada, however hotpot is a chinese cooking method that is very famous across Asia. It consists of gathering many people around a single pot where everyone will cook food like meat and mushrooms into a broth. Here the hotpot is sugary, grass jelly, mochi taro balls, sago and copious amounts of milk tea. It can be served warm or cold so it can good for any time of the year to eat a dessert with many friends

You might be more familiar with the traditional croissant found in any bakery is France however, it's in Singapore that can be found the croissant bubble tea! This flakey-buttery creation enveloping a gooey taro centre is very well liked and shows a new side of the traditional day to day croissant.

The bubble tea sandwich found in California is a crispy exterior encasing the boba and condensed milk for creamy crunches in every bite. A good snack to eat on the go, it can surely become your new favorite guilty pleasure if you just try a bite!

The bubble tea tarts can be found in Singapore, they consist of buttercream cakes customised with sugary boba on the top. It's a new type of tart that can change you from the usual strawberry or raspberry one.


Surely you might not want to travel around the world only to eat bubble tea in its various forms however, you can easily see the many ways that bubble tea can be customised in order to eat and discover this very well liked trend. And if one of these ideas raises your interest why not try to make it at home?
